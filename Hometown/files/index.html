Omsk: The Heart of Western Siberia

Omsk is a major Russian city located in the southwestern part of Siberia, serving as the administrative center of Omsk Oblast. Situated at the confluence of the Irtysh and Om Rivers, it is historically significant, having been founded in 1716 as a fortress to protect the southern frontiers of the Russian Empire. Today, Omsk is the second-largest city in Siberia (after Novosibirsk) and functions as a crucial industrial, cultural, and educational hub. It is a vital transport link, positioned directly on the Trans-Siberian Railway. The local economy is driven by large petrochemical, machinery, and aerospace industries. Culturally, Omsk is known for its historic architecture, numerous theaters, and museums. Notably, the city holds a place in literary history as the location where the famous Russian author Fyodor Dostoevsky spent four years in a penal servitude prison in the 1850s, an experience that heavily influenced his writing.
